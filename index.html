FAQs

Who's organising this?

The Space Hopper Challenge is being organised by TickTheBucket and London Flashmob.

TickTheBucket is a bucketlist themed social network dedicated to helping people fund, organise and achieve their dreams.

London Flashmob is the leading organiser of spontaneous events in the city, and has been featured on ITV, BBC News, the Londonist, Time Out London and London Live. Join the mailing list, like on Facebook or follow on Twitter.

When is the event?

Bank Holiday Monday, 29th May 2017 at 2.30pm. Doors open at 12pm so come armed with a picnic and some friends and make a day of it! You will need to arrive by 2pm at the latest in order to pick up your space hopper.

Where is it taking place?

At King's House Sports Ground, near Barnes Bridge, a beautiful spot along the Thames between Barnes Bridge and Chiswick stations - a 20 minute ride from Waterloo.

What is the current world record?

Currently the world record for the most people simultaneously bouncing in one place on a space hopper stands at 2,943. We don't plan to beat that record, we plan to SMASH it!
